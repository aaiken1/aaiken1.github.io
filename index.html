I am an associate professor of finance at Elon University in Elon, NC. My research interests include asset management, financial institutions, and performance measurement, with a particular focus on hedge funds. You can find my SSRN page here.
My co-authors and I have a paper on hedge fund reporting biases that was published in the Review of Financial Studies. Another paper on the use of discretionary liquidity restrictions by hedge funds was published in the Journal of Financial Economics. Our work on how funds-of-hedge-funds form their portfolios is published in Management Science. Work on the influence of ideology on the investment decisions of members of Congress was also published in Management Science.
Most recently, our paper on how the amount of capital under “lockup” at hedge funds affects their ability to exploit well-known equity anomalies, as well as own illiquid assets, was published at the Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis. Work on the communication choices of activist investors was published at the Journal of Corporate Finance.
Our research has been featured by The Economist, The Financial Times, Alpha, and Reuters.
I received a Ph.D. in Finance from Arizona State University (2010), a Masters in Economics from Duke University (2005), and a B.S. in Business Administration (Finance) from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill (2000). After graduating from UNC, I worked for the UNC Management Company, the endowment arm of the university and have been a CFA Charterholder since 2003. I am currently a member of the University of North Carolina at Greensboro Investment Fund Board.
Interests. Asset Management, Financial Institutions, Hedge Funds
